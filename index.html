monkeys

facts aboout monkeys:

Monkeys are primates, which means they are related to mammals as humans apes and lemurs.

There are over 260 different species of monkeys in the world, they are classified into two groups: New World monkeys and Old World monkeys.

Monkeys come in a variety of sizes, from the tiny pygmy marmoset, which weighs around 3.5 ounces, to the male mandrill, which can weigh up to 77 pounds. Monkeys have different diets. While most species are herbivorous, feeding on fruits, leaves, flowers, and nuts, some monkeys also eat insects, small animals, eggs, and even other monkeys. Apes are not monkeys.
